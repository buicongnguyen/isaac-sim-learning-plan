Problem Space

The main direction is perceptive humanoid locomotion over unstructured terrain. This means moving from blind walking toward policies that use onboard perception, terrain-aware training, and sim-to-real evaluation.

Leading direction

Perception + Locomotion

Depth, terrain geometry, and proprioception are used directly by the policy.

Target paper

Hiking in the Wild

Focuses on scalable perceptive parkour and hiking for humanoids. The key ideas to reproduce are depth-conditioned policy learning, terrain edge safety, and flat patch command generation.
